After 13 years as a Scientific Officer at BARI, stepping into the University of Western Australia felt like entering a different world — not just geographically, but intellectually.

## The Research

My Master's dissertation focused on marker-assisted selection in apple breeding. We analyzed 192 apple accessions using SSR and SCAR markers, applying population genetics statistics (Ho, He, PIC, HWE, Na, Ne) via GenAlEx 6.5 to understand genetic diversity and structure.

The work bridges two things I care deeply about:

1. **Applied breeding** — making real improvements to crops that farmers grow
2. **Molecular precision** — using DNA markers to select for traits we cannot easily see

## What I Learned at UWA

Working with Dr. Michael Considine and Dr. Sultan Mia gave me something I hadn't fully had before: space to think rigorously. Australian academic culture encourages questioning assumptions — even your supervisor's assumptions.

The library at UWA became a second home. Perth's quiet, the weather, the intellectual openness — all of it created conditions I found genuinely liberating.

## The Road to a PhD

This research is being prepared for submission to *Tree Genetics & Genomes*. Meanwhile, I am actively pursuing a PhD for 2027 intake, with UWA, ANU, and several Sydney-area universities as targets.

The question I want to pursue for a PhD: **how do we use population genomics to accelerate breeding decisions in underresourced crops?**

## What Comes Next

The immediate steps:

- Improve PTE score to 79+ for Australian PR points
